---

### 🧪 Research — King's College London
**Dr. Geoff Bird's Social Neuroscience Laboratory**

Investigated the social abilities of the right temporoparietal junction (rTPJ) using transcranial direct current stimulation (tDCS) to establish whether the rTPJ improves self-other control ability. Designed and tested a computerized imitation-inhibition task measuring error data and response times of instructed finger lifts when participants were presented with compatible and incompatible task-irrelevant stimuli. Participants with stimulated rTPJ scored better in imitation inhibition compared to the control mid-occipital site.

---

Explored the reflective aspect of self-consciousness and its role in social cognition, with a focus on theory-theory in terms of reflective and explicit self-representation, and its relationship with emotions and embodied cognition.
